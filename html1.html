Students and Teachers relationships

Strong relationships with teachers and school staff can greatly improve the motivation level of students and therefore promote learning. Students who have access to stronger relationships are more academically engaged, have stronger social skills, and experience more positive behavior. Unfortunately, many students do not have this experience. A survey of 25,400 sixth through twelfth grade students in a large, diverse district found that less than a third of high school students had a strong relationship with their teachers, and that figure dropped to 16% by the time students reached twelfth grade. . degree. Students from low-income backgrounds report even fewer strong relationships with their teachers.
